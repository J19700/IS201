Hey y'all! I love doing stop-motion and have been doing it for quite a bit of time now (approaching 10 years!). I primarily animate with Lego. Simply put, I take a picture, move the figure slightly, take another picture, and move the figure again. I shoot at 15 frames per second most of the time, meaning it takes 15 pictures to create one second of action. So, it often takes as much as 2-3 hours of work to produce 15-20 seconds of animation. I continually experiment with camera settings, lighting, and tools, like using small metal rigs to hold minifigures in the air, or sticky tack to stick them to difficult surfaces. Despite being time-consuming, the final product is immensely satisfying to watch.
